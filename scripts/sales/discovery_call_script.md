# TorqueLeads Discovery Call Script
*For booked Calendly calls with diesel shop owners*

---

## PRE-CALL PREP (5 min before)

- [ ] Pull up their shop website/Facebook
- [ ] Check Google reviews (look for fleet-related mentions)
- [ ] Have their local fleet list ready (from lead gen data)
- [ ] Open their area on Google Maps
- [ ] Have example ads/results ready to share

---

## OPENING (2-3 min)

> "Hey [NAME], good to connect. Thanks for taking the time. Before we dive in — how's the shop today? Busy?"

*(Let them talk. Build rapport. Shop owners love talking about their day.)*

> "Good to hear. So listen, I've got about 30 minutes blocked for us. Here's what I'm thinking — I want to learn a little bit about your shop and what's working (and what's not) for bringing in new business. Then I'll show you exactly what we do and how it might help. Sound good?"

---

## DISCOVERY QUESTIONS (10 min)

**Current State:**
1. > "Tell me about your shop. How long have you been in business?"

2. > "What's your bread and butter? Mostly fleet work, retail, mix of both?"

3. > "How many bays you running?"

4. > "What's a typical repair ticket look like for you on a fleet truck?"

**Pain Points:**
5. > "How are you getting new customers right now?"
   *(Listen for: word of mouth, repeat business, nothing, tried marketing)*

6. > "What have you tried in the past for marketing? What worked, what didn't?"

7. > "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about your business right now, what would it be?"

**Goals:**
8. > "Where do you want to be in 12 months? More bays? Bigger tickets? More fleet accounts?"

9. > "What would 5 new fleet accounts mean for your business?"

---

## TRANSITION TO PITCH

> "Alright, I've got a good picture of where you're at. Let me tell you what we do and why I think it could work for you."

---

## THE PITCH (10 min)

### Your Story
> "So quick background on me — I ran a heavy-duty diesel shop in California for 12 years. Did about $16 million in revenue before I sold it. I know this business inside and out."

> "When I was running the shop, I tried every marketing thing out there. SEO, Google Ads, mailers, you name it. Most of it didn't work. The one thing that actually moved the needle was targeted social media ads — specifically Facebook and Instagram."

### The Problem
> "Here's the thing most shop owners don't realize: SEO is dying. With AI search and zero-click results, people aren't clicking through to websites like they used to. And Google Ads? Those little sidebar ads? Nobody clicks those either."

> "But you know what everyone's still doing? Scrolling Facebook and Instagram. Including fleet managers. Including owner-operators. Including their wives and kids."

### The Solution
> "What we do is run hyper-targeted Meta ads that put your shop in front of every trucking company, fleet manager, and logistics outfit within 50 miles of you. Not just the owner — their whole network sees it."

> "It's not SEO. It's not hoping someone Googles 'diesel repair near me.' It's putting your shop directly in front of people who need your services, whether they're looking for you right now or not."

### The Proof
> "Let me show you something..."

*(Share screen — show their local fleet data)*

> "I pulled the FMCSA data for your area. There are [NUMBER] registered carriers within [X] miles of your shop. These are companies with DOT numbers, active trucks, and repair needs. Most of them aren't loyal to any shop yet."

*(Show example ad creative)*

> "Here's an example of the kind of ads we run. Simple, direct, speaks their language."

*(If you have results, share them)*

> "We've seen shops get [X] leads in the first month, at about [$X] per lead. For shops like yours, that usually means [X] new fleet accounts."

---

## HANDLE OBJECTIONS

**"How much does it cost?"**
> "It depends on your area and goals, but most shops invest somewhere between $1,500-3,000/month including ad spend. The key number is ROI — we're typically seeing 5-10x return. One new fleet account usually pays for 6 months of this."

**"I need to think about it"**
> "Totally get it. What specifically are you thinking over? Is it the investment, the time, or something else?"

**"I've been burned by marketing before"**
> "I hear that a lot. What happened? ... Yeah, that's exactly why I built this. Most marketing agencies don't understand this industry. I do. I ran a shop. I know what works and what doesn't."

**"I don't have time to manage this"**
> "You don't have to. We handle everything — the ads, the creative, the targeting. All you do is answer the phone when it rings. That's it."

**"Let me talk to my partner/wife"**
> "Of course. What questions do you think they'll have? Maybe I can help you answer them now so you're prepared."

---

## CLOSE (5 min)

### Trial Close
> "Based on what we've talked about, does this sound like something that could help [SHOP NAME]?"

*(Wait for response)*

### Direct Close
> "Here's what I'd suggest. Let's get started this week. I'll build out your first campaign, we'll go live, and within 2 weeks you'll see exactly what kind of response you're getting. No long-term contracts — if it's not working, we stop."

> "To get started, I just need a few things from you: some photos of your shop, your logo, and we'll set up the payment. Takes about 15 minutes."

> "Want to knock that out right now while we're on?"

### If They Need Time
> "I understand. Here's what I'll do — I'll send you a follow-up email with everything we talked about. Take a day or two, talk to [partner], and let's reconnect [DAY]. What time works?"
