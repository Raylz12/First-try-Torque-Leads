# TorqueLeads Cold Call Script
*For outbound calls to diesel repair shop owners*

---

## OPENING (10 seconds)

**Gatekeeper:**
> "Hey, this is [YOUR NAME] calling for the owner. Is [OWNER NAME] around?"

**Owner answers:**
> "Hey [OWNER NAME], this is [YOUR NAME] with TorqueLeads. I help diesel shops fill their bays with fleet work. Got 30 seconds?"

**If they say no:**
> "No problem. When's a better time to catch you?"

---

## HOOK (20 seconds)

> "Quick reason I'm calling — I ran a heavy-duty shop in California for 12 years, did about $16 million before I sold it. These days I help shops like yours get in front of fleet managers and owner-operators who actually need diesel work done."

> "Most marketing guys try to sell you SEO or Google Ads. That stuff's dying. We run targeted Meta ads that put your shop in front of every trucking company, logistics outfit, and fleet manager within 50 miles of you — and their families see it too."

---

## QUALIFY (30 seconds)

**Ask these questions:**

1. > "How are you getting new fleet customers right now?"
   *(Listen for: word of mouth, repeat business, nothing)*

2. > "What's your average repair ticket on a fleet truck?"
   *(Looking for: $800-3000+)*

3. > "If I could put your shop in front of 50+ fleet companies in your area this month, would that be worth a conversation?"

---

## HANDLE OBJECTIONS

**"I don't have time"**
> "I get it — you're in the shop. That's exactly why I handle everything. You just answer the phone when it rings. Can we do 15 minutes next week?"

**"I've tried marketing before, didn't work"**
> "Let me guess — SEO or Google Ads? Those are dead for this industry. We do something completely different. 15 minutes and I'll show you why."

**"I'm not interested"**
> "Fair enough. But before I let you go — are your bays full right now? Because if not, you're leaving money on the table every day those lifts sit empty."

**"How much does it cost?"**
> "Depends on your area and goals. But I'll tell you this — most of my shops see a 5-10x return on what they spend. Let's get on a call and I'll give you real numbers."

**"Send me some info"**
> "I can do that, but honestly the info doesn't do it justice. Give me 15 minutes on a call and I'll show you exactly how it works for shops in your area. What's your calendar look like tomorrow?"

---

## CLOSE (Book the call)

> "Here's what I'd like to do — let's jump on a quick 30-minute call where I show you exactly how this works. I'll pull up the fleet companies in your area, show you the ads we'd run, and give you real numbers. No pressure, no contracts on that call. Just information."

> "I've got [DAY] at [TIME] or [DAY] at [TIME]. Which works better?"

**After they pick a time:**
> "Perfect. I'm going to send you a calendar invite right now to [EMAIL]. You'll get a reminder before the call. Looking forward to it, [NAME]."

---

## VOICEMAIL (30 seconds max)

> "Hey [NAME], this is [YOUR NAME] with TorqueLeads. I help diesel shops fill their bays with fleet work — I actually ran a $16 million heavy-duty shop myself before I started this. 

> I've got some info on the fleet companies in your area that I think you'd want to see. Give me a call back at [PHONE NUMBER]. Again, that's [YOUR NAME], [PHONE NUMBER]. Talk soon."

---

## KEY PRINCIPLES

1. **Talk like a shop owner, not a marketer** — No jargon. No "synergy" or "leverage." Just straight talk.

2. **Lead with credibility** — The $16M shop experience is your best hook. Use it early.

3. **Create urgency** — Empty bays = lost money. Make them feel that.

4. **Always book a call** — Never settle for "send me info." Push for the meeting.

5. **Be brief** — Shop owners are busy. Respect their time and get to the point.

---

## TRACKING

Log every call:
- Shop name
- Owner name
- Outcome (booked/callback/not interested/no answer)
- Best callback time
- Notes
